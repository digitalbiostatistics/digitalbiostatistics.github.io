Professor Matabuena's teaching and research interests span digital biostatistics and epidemiology, with emphasis on functional and distributional data analysis, uncertainty quantification (e.g., conformal prediction), machine-learning-based survival modeling, and multilevel methods for continuous, high-frequency biosignals. He develops methodology for random objects in metric spaces, interpretable machine learning, and rigorous study design for complex surveys and clinical cohorts. His applied work spans diabetes-particularly continuous glucose monitoring and glucodensity profiles-physical activity and aging using accelerometry and smartphone data, and population-health analyses leveraging nationally representative datasets. He is currently focused on advancing methods for longitudinal digital clinical data from wearables and smartphones and on integrating these streams with complementary modalities-such as genetic and other omics data-to improve prediction, inference, and decision-making in real-world healthcare settings.
